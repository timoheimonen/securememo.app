About securememo.app

securememo.app is for short private notes. Memo contents are encrypted in your browser before upload, and the server only stores ciphertext plus the metadata needed to expire and delete the memo.

Create Secure Memo

What makes it private

Encrypted in your browser

Your memo is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it leaves your device. We only store ciphertext.

Passwords stay on your device

The encryption password is generated in your browser. It's never sent to or stored on our servers.

Deleted after read or expiry

After a successful read, the browser confirms deletion with the server. Expired memos are cleaned up automatically.

No accounts, no tracking

You don't need to sign up. We don't use cookies, advertising trackers, third-party analytics, or behavioral profiling. We keep only limited aggregate operational metrics needed to run and protect the service.

What the server stores

The server is designed to handle delivery and cleanup, not memo contents.

Stored temporarily

  • Encrypted memo ciphertext
  • Expiry time
  • Random memo ID
  • Deletion-token hash

Not stored

  • Plaintext memo contents
  • Encryption passwords
  • User accounts
  • Cookies, tracking identifiers, or advertising data

How to share a memo

  1. 1

    Write it

    Type your note and pick an expiry time. Your browser encrypts it before uploading.

  2. 2

    Share separately

    Send the link and the password through different channels for extra safety.

  3. 3

    Read it once

    The recipient opens the link, enters the password, and the memo decrypts in their browser.
