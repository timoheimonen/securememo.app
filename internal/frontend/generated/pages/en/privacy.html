1. Overview

securememo.app is a privacy-focused service for creating encrypted memos that are deleted after successful read confirmation or expiration. The service is designed to collect as little information as possible: no accounts, no cookies, no trackers, no advertising, and no behavioral profiling.

Memo content is encrypted in your browser before it is sent to the server. The server stores encrypted memo data temporarily, but it does not receive the plaintext memo content or the memo password and cannot decrypt memos for you.

2. Controller and Contact

The data controller for securememo.app is Timo Heimonen.

Privacy contact: timo.heimonen@proton.me

3. How the Service Works

  • You type a memo in your browser.
  • Your browser generates a random password and a deletion token.
  • Your browser encrypts the memo and deletion token using Web Crypto API encryption before upload.
  • The server stores only the encrypted memo payload, a memo ID, an expiration time, and a hash of the deletion token.
  • The password is shown only in your browser. It is not sent to or stored by securememo.app.
  • The recipient must receive both the memo URL and the password. They should be shared through separate channels.
  • After the memo is successfully decrypted in the recipient's browser, the browser sends the deletion token to confirm deletion.
  • Memos are also deleted after expiration. Available expiration options are 8 hours, 24 hours, 48 hours, 1 week, and 30 days.

4. Information We Process

We process only the information needed to operate, protect, and maintain the service:

  • Encrypted memo data: the encrypted memo payload created in your browser.
  • Memo metadata: memo ID, creation time, expiration time, and deletion-token hash.
  • Rate-limit data: short-lived abuse-prevention counters keyed by hashed client IP address, action, and time window.
  • Operational metrics: aggregate or technical data used to monitor service health, such as request counts, response status counts, latency, error rates, bandwidth, uptime, and cleanup counts.
  • Request metadata: limited technical metadata may be processed by the application or infrastructure to route requests, apply security controls, and diagnose operational issues. This can include IP address, time, requested path, response status, user agent, origin, and referrer headers.
  • Contact data: if you contact us, we process your email address and message content so we can respond.

Operational metrics are used to understand whether the server is working correctly. They are not used to track individual visitors across time or sites.

5. Information We Do Not Collect

  • We do not receive or store plaintext memo content.
  • We do not receive or store memo passwords.
  • We do not create user accounts.
  • We do not use cookies.
  • We do not use tracking pixels, advertising tags, behavioral analytics, fingerprinting scripts, or third-party marketing beacons.
  • We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data.
  • We do not use memo data or operational metrics for advertising or profiling.

6. Purposes and Legal Bases

If GDPR or similar data protection law applies, we rely on these legal bases:

  • Providing the service: processing encrypted memos and memo metadata is necessary to provide the memo-sharing service requested by you.
  • Security and abuse prevention: rate limiting, request metadata, and operational security processing are based on our legitimate interest in protecting the service and its users.
  • Operational monitoring: metrics are processed based on our legitimate interest in keeping the service reliable, secure, and maintainable.
  • Responding to contact: contact data is processed to respond to your request.
  • Legal compliance: limited data may be processed or disclosed when required by applicable law.

7. Cookies, Tracking, and Browser Storage

  • securememo.app does not set, read, or depend on cookies.
  • securememo.app does not use localStorage, sessionStorage, IndexedDB, or persistent browser identifiers for memo data.
  • securememo.app does not use analytics or advertising trackers.
  • If you click a copy button, your browser may place the memo URL or password in your device clipboard. Clipboard handling is controlled by your browser and operating system.

We do not enable Cloudflare features for this service that require visitor cookies.

8. Cloudflare and Infrastructure Processing

securememo.app is served behind Cloudflare. Cloudflare helps route traffic, protect the service from abuse, provide TLS termination, and produce security and operational metrics.

When you visit securememo.app, Cloudflare may process technical data such as IP address, request metadata, security events, and network data as part of providing its services. We use Cloudflare for infrastructure and security, not for advertising or behavioral tracking.

Because memo content is encrypted in your browser before upload, Cloudflare does not receive plaintext memo content or memo passwords from the service flow.

9. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data. We disclose or make data available only in limited situations:

  • Infrastructure providers: Cloudflare and hosting infrastructure process data needed to deliver, secure, and operate the service.
  • Legal requirements: we may disclose limited data if required by valid legal process or applicable law.
  • Protection of the service: data may be used to investigate abuse, attacks, or attempts to compromise the service.
  • Business transfer: if the service is transferred to another operator, data may transfer only as necessary and subject to this notice or a replacement notice with equivalent or stronger privacy commitments.

10. Retention and Deletion

  • Encrypted memos: retained until successful read confirmation or expiration, with a maximum selected expiration of 30 days.
  • Expired memos: removed by automatic cleanup jobs that run periodically.
  • Rate-limit data: kept only for the active rate-limit window and removed by cleanup. Current windows are short, including minute and hour windows.
  • Operational metrics: kept only as long as needed to monitor service health, troubleshoot issues, and protect the service.
  • Contact data: kept as long as needed to respond to and handle your message, unless a longer period is required for legitimate recordkeeping or legal reasons.

After a memo is deleted, securememo.app does not provide app-level recovery. Because the service never stores the password, we cannot decrypt or restore memo content for you.

11. Security

  • Memos are encrypted in the browser before upload.
  • The server stores encrypted payloads and deletion metadata, not plaintext memo content or passwords.
  • Deletion confirmation uses a deletion token stored inside the encrypted memo payload and a server-side hash for verification.
  • The service uses security headers, origin checks, input validation, size limits, timing protections, and rate limiting.
  • SQLite secure-delete mode is enabled to reduce recoverable deleted content in the database file.

No online service can be guaranteed perfectly secure. You are responsible for sharing memo URLs and passwords through appropriate separate channels and for protecting devices and browsers used to create or read memos.

12. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection to processing of your personal data. You may also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

Because securememo.app does not use accounts and intentionally avoids persistent identifiers, we may not be able to identify data as belonging to a particular person. If we cannot reasonably verify your identity or locate data connected to you, we will explain that. We will still handle privacy requests where feasible and required by law.

13. Children

The service is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data through the service, contact us so we can review and delete it where possible.

14. International Transfers

The service is operated from Finland, and infrastructure providers may process technical data in other countries. Where applicable, transfers are handled through provider agreements and legal transfer mechanisms. Cloudflare may process network and security data through its global network.

15. Changes
